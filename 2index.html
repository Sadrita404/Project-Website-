How to Make a Drone

Project by Sadrita Neogi
Making this project was quite an adventure! After hours of tweaking and calibrating, I was finally able to make this work. I'm sharing my experience and mistakes here so you can build your own high-performance micro drone.
Step 0

Supplies & Parts

4x Brushed 3.7V 720 Micro Motors
2 CW and 2 CCW Propellers
3.7v LiPo (300mAh - 500mAh)
3D Printed or Acrylic Frame
HM-10 Bluetooth Module
MPU6050 Sensor Module
MT3608 DC-DC Boost Converter
Atmega328p / Arduino Pro Mini
SI2302 Mosfets (x4)
SS12 Diodes (x4)

Step 1

The Schematic

This schematic can drive up to 6 motors, but we only need 4 for this Quadcopter build.

Motor Location Arduino Pin Mapping
Front Left (M1)Digital Pin D3
Front Right (M3)Digital Pin D6
Rear Right (M4)Digital Pin D9
Rear Left (M2)Digital Pin D5
Pro Tip: Using an Arduino Pro Mini is recommended if you aren't comfortable soldering SMD chips like the Atmega328p.

PCB Design & Fabrication

Step 3

Wiring & Power

Ensure motor polarities are correct to avoid flips on takeoff.

Voltage Safety: Adjust the MT3608 potentiometer to 5V before connecting it to the Arduino.
Step 5

Calibration (MultiWiiConf)

Connect via COM port and click CALIB_ACC while the drone is level.

Step 6

Final Result

The build is complete! Lightweight and agile.
